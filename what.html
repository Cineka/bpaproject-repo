What are CO2 Emissions?

Greenhouse gases trap heat in the atmosphere and make the planet progressively hotter. In the United States, the majority of the emissions are from burning fossil fuels for electricity, heating, and transportation.

The main gases that cause problems in the atmosphere are Carbon Dioxide, Methane, Nitrous Oxide, and fluorinated gases.

  • Carbon Dioxide is the most common greenhouse gas. This gas is produced when things like coal, natural gas, oil, solid waste, trees, and other biological materials.

  • Methane is the second most common greenhouse gas in the atmosphere. Methane is produced when things like coal, oil, and natural gas are transported and produced. As well as come from livestock and other agricultural practices, land use and by the decay of organic waste.

  • Nitrous Oxide is another Greenhouse gas. It is emitted during agricultural, land use, industrial activities, burning of fossil fuels and solid waste such as trees, as well as during treatment of wastewater.

  • Fluorinated gases are gases that are produced during many industrial processes. These types of gases are typically emitted in small amounts, but since they are potent greenhouse gases, they are sometimes referred to as high global warming potential gases.
